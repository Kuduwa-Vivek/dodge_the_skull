Game Overview

Total Cards: 104
Players: Minimum 4, Maximum 10
Points: Red Skull 1 Point, White Skull 3 Point
Objective: Collect the fewest skulls points by the end of the game
üíÄ

Game Setup

  • Shuffle all 104 cards.
  • Each player receives 10 cards.
  • No player may have more than 10 cards.
  • Place 4 cards face-up on the table.
  • Arrange them vertically to form 4 columns.
üíÄ

How to Play (Each Round)

1

Card Selection

Each player secretly selects 1 card from their hand. Place the selected card face-down on the table. Do not reveal it until everyone has selected.

2

Reveal & Order

All players reveal their chosen cards at the same time. Arrange the revealed cards in descending order by number. Ignore skulls while ordering - Only numbers matter here.

3

Place Cards in Columns

Start with the highest revealed card.

For each card:

  • Place it in the column where the top card is just higher than it.
  • The numbers in a column must always go downward (descending).
  • Choose the column where the number difference is the smallest.
  • Keep placing cards one by one until any column gets a 6th card.

When a column reaches the 6th card, the penalty rule applies.

4

Collect Cards (Penalty)

If your card becomes the 6th card in any column: You must collect the 5 cards above it from that column. Keep those cards aside (these are your penalty cards). Your card becomes the new top card of that column.

5

Repeat

Continue the above steps until all players run out of cards.

Special Case Rule

No Valid Column Available

If a player's revealed card cannot be placed in any of the 4 columns because:

  • The card's number is higher than the top card of all columns, or
  • It does not fit the descending order rule for any column

‚û§ Forced Column Pickup

The player must choose any one of the 4 columns. The player collects all cards from that chosen column as penalty cards. These collected cards are kept aside and must not be mixed back into the deck. The player's selected card is then placed as the new top card of that column.

üíÄ

Rounds & Game End

  • The game is played for 10 rounds.
  • After all rounds are completed: Count the skulls on the cards you collected from the table.
  • The player with the fewest skulls wins.
üíÄ

Tie Rule

If two or more players tie for the lowest skull count: Only the tied players play the game again. The winner of this replay is the final winner.

üíÄ

Winning Condition

Survive ten rounds. Count your skulls points. The least cursed player wins.
